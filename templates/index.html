With the advent of the Internet of Things (IoT), monitoring and managing water quality have reached new heights of efficiency and accuracy. Water quality mapping involves the assessment and visualization of various parameters that determine the purity, safety, and health of water resources. It encompasses the analysis of factors such as pH levels, turbidity, temperature, etc. IoT technologies have revolutionized the process of monitoring water quality. Sensors and smart devices are deployed across water bodies, collecting real-time data. These sensors continuously gather information and transmit it to central systems for analysis While IoT-based water quality mapping offers numerous advantages, challenges persist, including sensor calibration, data security, and ensuring data integrity. However, continuous advancements in sensor technology and data analytics promise a brighter future for accurate and sustainable water quality management. In conclusion, the integration of IoT in water quality mapping is a crucial step towards ensuring the availability of clean and safe water resources, essential for sustaining life and supporting a healthy ecosystem.
